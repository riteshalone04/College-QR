Introduction and Facility Overview

Welcome and Purpose

Welcome! This lab is designed for hands-on experience, bridging the gap between digital logic theory and practical circuit implementation. Our goal is to provide a safe, structured environment to solidify your foundational understanding of **Electronics Devices & Circuit**.

Lab Layout and Infrastructure

The lab is approximately **750 sq. ft.** (30 ft x 25 ft) and optimized for collaborative learning. The main area features **two giant workbenches** with ergonomic seating. The facility is well-ventilated, and two private staff cabins are strategically located for immediate guidance.

Safety Rules and Emergency Procedures

Strict adherence to safety guidelines is required for all personnel.

General Safety Protocol

  • **Storage:** Store bags in designated areas to prevent tripping hazards.
  • **No Food/Drink:** Absolutely no food or drinks are allowed on the workbenches.
  • **Reporting:** Report faulty equipment, damaged cords, or any injury immediately to lab staff.
  • **Tidiness:** Ensure your work area is clean and tidy before leaving.

Electrical Safety and Emergency

  • **Inspect Cords:** Always inspect power cords (CRO, DMM) for damage before use.
  • **Check Polarity:** Double-check Vcc and GND polarity on all components before applying power.
  • **Fire Extinguisher:** CO2 extinguisher (for electrical fires) is located at [Location].
  • **First-Aid:** The First-Aid Kit is maintained in [Location].
  • **Main Power:** The Main Power Switch is located at [Location] for emergencies.

Equipment Inventory and Usage Guide

Core Workstation Setup

Each station has the fundamental tools necessary for assembling, powering, and testing transistor-based and IC circuits.

Equipment Type Purpose / Standard Quantity (Typical)
**DC Power Supply**Provides stable DC voltage (e.g., +5V for TTL or variable for BJT/FET bias).1 per station
**Breadboard**Non-soldering platform for temporary circuit assembly.1 per station
**Digital Multimeter (DMM)**Measures voltage (DC/AC), current, and checks continuity/resistance.1 per station
**CRO/Oscilloscope**Visualizes and measures time-varying signals (e.g., sine, square wave).1 per station
**Function Generator**Generates various input signals (sine, square, triangle waves).Shared/Dedicated
**Components Kit**Includes Diodes, BJTs, FETs, Op-Amps, Resistors, Capacitors, and ICs.Per experiment

Key Measuring and Visualization Instruments

Accurate measurement is critical. You will use the **CRO** to visualize analog and digital waveforms and timing. The **Digital Multimeter (DMM)** is used for static voltage checks and continuity. The **Function Generator** provides stable input signals (AC/DC) for testing amplifiers and oscillators.

Specialized training tools, such as Experimental T.V.s, are reserved for advanced application experiments. Specific instruction on their use will be provided by staff.

Lab Procedures and Experiments

In-Lab Workflow

  1. **Pre-Lab:** Read the procedure and complete all pre-lab calculations (Staff will check readiness).
  2. **Assembly:** Build your circuit neatly on the breadboard.
  3. **Power Check:** **MUST** get verification from staff before applying power.
  4. **Testing:** Verify circuit function using the CRO and DMM.
  5. **Troubleshooting:** Verify Power, Connections, and IC Orientation in that order if the circuit fails.
  6. **Post-Lab:** Turn off all equipment, disassemble the circuit, clean your station, and submit your report.
